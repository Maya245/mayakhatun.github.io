## Research Overview

My research focuses on the development and application of theoretical and computational chemistry approaches to understand the structure, stability, and properties of atomically precise nanoclusters. By combining global optimization algorithms, density functional theory (DFT), excited-state electronic structure methods, and data-driven approaches, my work aims to explore complex chemical spaces and establish structure–property relationships in functional nanomaterials.

## Automated Global Optimization of Nanoclusters

Atomically precise nanoclusters exhibit complex potential energy landscapes
with numerous possible structural arrangements. My research involves the
development and application of automated global optimization strategies
for efficient exploration of nanocluster chemical space and identification
of stable structures. The developed computational approaches enable systematic discovery of
low-energy nanocluster structures and provide insights into the relationship
between atomic arrangement, bonding, and stability.


## Density Functional Theory and Computational Method Development

Reliable computational methods are essential for accurate prediction of
nanocluster properties. My research focuses on evaluating the performance
of density functional approximations and semiempirical methods for describing
structural, energetic, and electronic properties of nanoclusters.

These studies provide guidelines for selecting appropriate computational
methods for modeling complex nanoscale systems.


## Electronic, Optical, and Chiroptical Properties of DNA-Stabilized Silver Nanoclusters

DNA-stabilized silver nanoclusters represent an emerging class of functional
nanomaterials with tunable electronic and optical properties. Using
ground-state DFT, linear-response TDDFT, electronic structure analysis,
and spectroscopy simulations, my research investigates how atomic structures,
ligand environments, and chemical modifications control their optical and
chiroptical responses.

## Computational Approaches

My research combines several theoretical and computational approaches:

- Density Functional Theory (DFT)
- Wave Function based Theory (WFT)
- Finding cost-accuracy balanced methodology
- Linear-Response Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (LR-TDDFT)
- Global Optimization and Chemical Space Exploration
- Electronic Structure Analysis
- Potential Energy Surface Exploration
- Optical Absorption and Circular Dichroism Spectroscopy
- Bonding and Charge Analysis
